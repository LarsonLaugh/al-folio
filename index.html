Lixian Wang

Postdoc @ the University of Wuerzburg, PhD in Physics from Peking University

I am a researcher, working on quantum transport at Wuerzburg University, Germany. My interest lies at the intersection of physics, math, and software and a bit electronics.

I received my PhD in Physics at Peking University. I completed my undergraduate study 3-year in Physics and one-year in computer science at Northwest University (Xi’an).

My research has focused on novel phenomena in solid-state physics, like topological insulators, Dirac semimetals,etc.

During my research and spare time, I am passionate with writing software for multiple reasons:

  1. to implement mathematical algorithm to solve complex problems (counterflow)
  2. to power programmable hardware (Ardurio, Pi Pico) to build fun toy (pidML)
  3. to simulate a real physical system to assist the understanding of experimental obsesrvations (toyband),
  4. to facilitate data handling and analysis (SciData)
  5. to assist my research (arXivEng), study (personalLib) and stock investment (stocksync)
